Both <math xmlns:m="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" xmlns:md="http://cnx.rice.edu/mdml" xmlns="http://cnx.rice.edu/cnxml"><semantics><mrow><mrow><mi>ω</mi></mrow><mrow/></mrow><annotation encoding="StarMath 5.0"> size 12&#123;ω&#125; &#123;&#125;</annotation></semantics></math> and <math xmlns:m="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" xmlns:md="http://cnx.rice.edu/mdml" xmlns="http://cnx.rice.edu/cnxml"><semantics><mrow><mrow><mi>v</mi></mrow><mrow/></mrow><annotation encoding="StarMath 5.0"> size 12&#123;v&#125; &#123;&#125;</annotation></semantics></math> have directions (hence they are angular and linear  *velocities*, respectively). Angular velocity has only two directions with respect to the axis of rotation—it is either clockwise or counterclockwise.
