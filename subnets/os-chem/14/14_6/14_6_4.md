which can be written as

where p *K*<sub>a</sub> is the negative of the common logarithm of the ionization constant of the weak acid (p *K*<sub>a</sub> = −log  *K*<sub>a</sub>). This equation relates the pH, the ionization constant of a weak acid, and the concentrations of the weak acid and its salt in a buffered solution. Scientists often use this expression, called the  **Henderson-Hasselbalch equation**, to calculate the pH of buffer solutions. It is important to note that the “ *x* is small” assumption must be valid to use this equation.
