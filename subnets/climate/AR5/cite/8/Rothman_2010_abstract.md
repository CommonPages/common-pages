This note provides a brief review of the history and impact of the molecular spectroscopic database known as HITRAN (High Resolution Transmission). We reprint one of the articles describing this database (JQSRT 1998; 60: 665â€“710), although the series of these papers is among the most cited in the geosciences. HITRAN was established over three decades ago in response to the needs of simulating transmission in the terrestrial atmosphere, and it was enabled by new technology. However the applications of HITRAN, and the requirements on the database, have greatly expanded over the intervening years.
