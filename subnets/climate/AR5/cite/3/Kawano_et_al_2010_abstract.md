We studied the change in heat content in the Pacific Ocean using data from repeat hydrographic surveys. The most recent trans-Pacific surveys along 47째N and 179째E conducted in 2007 reveal that circumpolar deep water has warmed along 179째E as suggested by previous studies and also that the deep water was warmer along 47째N even since 1999. Simple estimates show that the heat content increased in the layer deeper than 4000m over almost the entire Pacific Ocean. The increase in heat content below 3000m was about 5% of the ocean-wide increase. Heat content changes in the deep layer are not uniform and a vertical profile of heat content change has a minimum at a depth around 3000m. The non-uniform distribution of heat content change in the mid- to bottom layer demonstrates the existence of overturn circulation and may indicate that this circulation is changing.
