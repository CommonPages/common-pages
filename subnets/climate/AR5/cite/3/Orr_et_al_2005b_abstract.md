The ocean's vast capacity to take up anthropogenic CO2 helps limit increases in atmospheric CO2, thus moderating climate. Unfortunately, this passive uptake also implies a reduction in surface ocean pH. Such ocean acidification occurs because CO2 reacts with water, increasing hydrogen ions; simultaneously, bicarbonate ions increase and carbonate ions decrease. Because passive ocean uptake cannot keep pace with increases of atmospheric CO2, there have been proposals to actively enhance ocean uptake of CO2. Both passive and active uptake of anthropogenic CO2 will also affect ocean biology via changes in ocean chemistry.
