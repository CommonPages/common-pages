A recent article by Rosenlof and Reid [2008] (hereinafter referred to as RR08) examines the relationship in the tropics between long‐term cooling in the stratosphere and warming of the sea surface, with special emphasis in the western Pacific. In the stratosphere, radiosonde observations seem to show that most of the cooling took place since the mid‐1990s, apparently coincident to a considerable extent with an increase in the rate of warming of sea surface temperatures (SSTs). A link was proposed by RR08 whereby the increase in SSTs leads to enhanced convection, which is responsible, dynamically, for cooling in the stratosphere.
