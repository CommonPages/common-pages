Deep-layer temperatures derived from satellite-borne microwave sensors since 1979 are revised (version 5.0) to account for 1) a change from microwave sounding units (MSUs) to the advanced MSUs (AMSUs) and 2) an improved diurnal drift adjustment for tropospheric products. AMSU data, beginning in 1998, show characteristics indistinguishable from the earlier MSU products. MSU-AMSU error estimates are calculated through comparisons with radiosonde-simulated bulk temperatures for the low-middle troposphere (TLT), midtroposphere (TMT), and lower stratosphere (TLS.) Monthly (annual) standard errors for global mean anomalies of TLT satellite temperatures are estimated at 0.10degreesC (0.07degreesC). The TLT (TMT) trend for January 1979 to April 2002 is estimated as +0.06degrees (+0.02degrees) +/- 0.05degreesC decade(-1) (95% confidence interval). Error estimates for TLS temperatures are less well characterized due to significant heterogeneities in the radiosonde data at high altitudes, though evidence is presented to suggest that since 1979 the trend is -0.51degrees +/- 0.10degreesC decade(-1).
