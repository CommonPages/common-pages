A map of Greenland in the 13th edition (2011) of the &lt;i&gt;Times Comprehensive Atlas of the World&lt;/i&gt; made headlines because the publisher's media release mistakenly stated that the permanent ice cover had shrunk 15% since the previous 10th edition (1999) revision. The claimed shrinkage was immediately challenged by glaciologists, then retracted by the publisher. Here we show: (1) accurate maps of ice extent based on 1978/87 aerial surveys and recent MODIS imagery; and (2) shrinkage at 0.019% a&lt;sup&gt;âˆ’1&lt;/sup&gt; in ~50 000 km&lt;sup&gt;2&lt;/sup&gt; of ice in a part of east Greenland that is shown as ice-free in the &lt;i&gt;Times Atlas&lt;/i&gt;.
