A locally one-dimensional model of heat and moisture transfer in the soil is described. The ground surface can be bare, vegetated, or snow-covered. Primary attention is given to possibly accurate calculation of heat and moisture diffusion, with allowance made for all three states of water and their phase transitions. The model was validated in a stand-alone mode (with observed data used as input parameters) by reproducing the soil freezing process and seasonal variations in the soil temperature and moisture characteristics. A comparative analysis of model results and available observational data showed sufficiently high quality of the model and a possibility of its use within atmospheric general circulation models.
