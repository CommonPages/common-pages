Future climate change projections are constructed from simulated numerical output from a small set of global climate models—samples of opportunity known as multi‐model ensembles. Climate models do not produce probabilities, nor are they perfect representations of the real climate, and there are complex inter‐relationships due to shared model features. This creates interesting statistical challenges for making inference about the real climate.
